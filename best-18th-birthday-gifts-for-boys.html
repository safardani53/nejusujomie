35 Best 18th Birthday Gifts For Boys 2021 | The Sun UK

September 19, 2024
blog

BUYING an 18th birthday gift is no easy task. Teenagers are notoriously picky, and often times annoyingly clued up on trends.

So unless you flat out ask them what they want, playing the guessing game is risky business. Luckily there are a few tried and tested ideas that won’t fail to impress.

Every 18 year old is of course different. But for a landmark birthday such as this, some classic gifts will always be appropriate.

And for the tech-obsessed teenager, there are more options than ever. From bluetooth speakers to headphones and smartwatches, the options are never ending.

Looking for inspiration? Here’s our round-up of the best 18th birthday gifts for boys.

Bluetooth speakers

Bluetooth speakers are a great gift, regardless of age. Easy to connect to phones, tablets and laptops, they offer far better, deeper sound than your device’s built in speakers.

They can also be taken to any room in the house, thanks to their small size and portability. Perfect for using while studying, or taking round a mate’s house, bluetooth speakers are an essential for music-loving 18 year olds and casual listeners alike.

Gaming gifts

A headset hits two birds with one stone. It gives the 18 year old in question an unrivalled gaming experience, with deep bass and immersive 3D sound quality. And, if you happen to live with them, it means you no longer have to endure their blaring TV.

It’s probably best, and far safer, to keep the colours simple and easy to wear if possible. Unless you’re 100% confident about the person you’re buying for’s taste, stick to white, black and simple blue or green accents.

Watch

A new watch will never not be a good 18th birthday present. While we would advise keeping it timeless and going for a classic, mechanical watch, the smartwatch boom can’t be ignored. And let’s face it, chances are most 18 year olds would take an Apple Watch over a Timex.

The latest Apple Watches will set you back, but they come with all the latest tech and sleek design that sets the California brand apart. Other smartwatches are of course readily available, and many have innovative features including full screen displays, video calling, sensors that can measure your bloody oxygen levels and way more.

If you do want to stay classic though, look out for understated, easy to wear watches that will stand the test of time. You can’t go wrong with Japanese brand Seiko, which produces a clean range of tool watches, and jokes aside, Timex has specialised in affordable designs since 1854.

Wallet

You can’t go wrong with gifting a wallet for someone’s 18th. Sure, it might not be the most exciting present, but a good quality leather wallet will get used every day, and should last years.

Again, stick to classic designs that will look as good now as they will in five years time. Think textured leather in black or brown, and turn to iconic brands including Barbour, John Lewis and Ralph Lauren. Headphones

It’s not an assumption to think that most 18 year olds will have a smartphone. But said smartphones mostly come equipped with sub-par headphones that don’t make the most of their audio capabilities.

Improve their sound by gifting upgraded headphones, which boast the necessary tech to get more out of music and video. Deeper bass, richer mid-tones and more defined sound can be found in cans from the likes of Beats, Sony and Bose.

Whether you go for over-ear or in-ear headphones is largely down to personal preference, so maybe try to gauge which style the person you’re buying for would prefer.

Shaving gear

Shaving is an essential part of every man’s grooming routine. Now the 18 year old in question has entered manhood, it’s likely time to either start shaving, or improve on existing technique.

Forget seven-bladed razors, for wet shaving, you only need one. A safety razor not only looks cool, it also offers about as close a shave as you can get. Often made from solid stainless steel, safety razors are a sustainable way to shave - they last for years and you simply replace the blade when it’s worn.

Don’t forget the shaving brush, too. Necessary to whip up a lather with a quality cream, brushes properly prepare the face for shaving. Use in a circular motion to soften the hair and open up the follicles, which allows for a closer shave. For those that want to maintain a beard or short stubble though, an electric razor is probably the way to go.
