Who is Bo Burnham?

Robert Pickering Burnham a.k.a. Bo Burnham is an American musician, songwriter, rapper, poet, comedian, filmmaker, and Youtuber.

His films frequently incorporate aspects of stand-up, musical, and sketch humor.

His comedic show Inside (2021) and the adolescent movie Eighth Grade (2018) have won praise from critics.

Following early success on YouTube, Burnham became well-known in the early 2010s for his caustic and subversive stand-up and musical comedy.

Between 2008 and 2013, he produced four comedic albums for Comedy Central Records, and three stand-up specials (Words, Words, Words (2010),. (2013) and the mockumentary series Zach Stone Is Gonna Be Famous for MTV in 2013.

In 2018, he created and directed the critically praised movie Eighth Grade after announcing a change in career in 2016.

He also oversees live comedy specials as a filmmaker. He starred in the Oscar-winning comedy-thriller Promising Young Woman.

Early Life of Bo Burnham

Bo Burnham was born on 21st August 1990 and is 32 years of age as of 2022. He was born in Hamilton, Massachusetts, United States.

He is the son of Scott Burnham, owner of a construction company, and Patricia Burnham, a hospice nurse.

His mother was interviewed on a 2014 episode of This American Life, which featured a story about her work.

Both of his older siblings, Pete and Samm, who are employed by the family’s construction business, are Samm’s older sisters.

Burnham was raised a Catholic and received a free education at St. John’s Preparatory School in Danvers, Massachusetts, where his mother was employed as the school’s nurse at the time.

He graduated in 2008 and was on the honor roll as well as active in theatre and the campus ministry program.

He was accepted to New York University’s Tisch School of the Arts to study experimental theatre but postponed his admission for a year to pursue it.

Bo Burnham Career

After receiving appreciation for his songs in school, Bo was full of confidence in 2006 and shared two videos with his far-flung friends and family.

These films were so well-liked that Break.com cloned them, which led to Bo’s meteoric rise to fame and a hundredfold increase in video views.

Bo was encouraged by the feedback to continue posting songs online, with the majority of them focusing on issues like racism, homophobia, physical disability, and other related issues.

Bo continued to make videos and his fan base kept expanding until he got his first major break.

Bo’s first major break came in the form of Comedy Central’s “The World Stands Up” in January 2008. Bo’s work was so entertaining that he later signed a four-record deal with them.

In a matter of months, Bo’s debut EP, titled “Bo Fo Sho,” became available for digital download. In 2009, he released “Bo Burnham,” his debut full-length album.

Bo has since performed in front of live crowds in various cities around the United States, including San Francisco and New York City, as well as abroad with performances in London and Montreal.

He was nominated for the Best Comedy Show award at the 2010 Edinburgh Comedy Awards for his performance in “Words, Words, Words.” Bo received the Panel Prize despite missing the award.

In October 2010, the show was released, and it received positive reviews.

More than a million people watched Bo’s second special, “What,” which was released on both Netflix and YouTube in 2013.
