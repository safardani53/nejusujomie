Cheltenham Festival stable tour - Gordon Elliott runs through his potential runners for this year's

September 07, 2024
blog

GORDON ELLIOTT is once again assembling a strong team for the Cheltenham Festival, starring Samcro and Apple's Jade.

Elliott amassed a super six winners to finish last year's Festival as top trainer and will be hoping for more of the same in March.

SAMCRO - BALLYMORE NOVICES’ HURDLE

“He’s a nice horse but he’s got to improve again, he’s done everything right so far.

“He’s got to step up again for Cheltenham and it will be the first time he’s travelled to race so that can often be a big ask too.

“I was nervous about going to Leopardstown with him as I thought would he have enough pace for two miles? But he just showed he can do it. He does everything in his stride and looks a very nice horse.

“We’re looking forward to going to Cheltenham and he ticks all the boxes.

“The Ballymore is the right race at the moment and the only reason that might change is if it came up bottomless and that’s not going to happen.”

APPLE’S JADE - OLBG MARES’ HURDLE

“She’s in great form, she’s looking like a bull at the moment to be honest and is mad for a run.

“People are saying the Champion or the Stayers but I’m a firm believer in that you have to go to the race you think you can win. I think it’s the best chance of our winner at the Festival so that’s the way she’ll go. 

“As far as training goes we’ve had a very clear run. 

“If I made one mistake with her all year I should have run her in the Irish Champion Hurdle. But hindsight is a great thing.”

CAUSE OF CAUSES - GLENFARCLAS CROSS COUNTRY CHASE

“He’s in great form, he loves it when the sun comes out at this time of the year and he seems to love Cheltenham.

“He’s been to school over the course the other week and that went well.

“The cross country and the Grand National has been the plan for him all season.”

CRACKING SMART - ALBERT BARTLETT NOVICES’ HURDLE

“He’ll go for the Albert Bartlett. He ran very well behind Next Destination in the Grade 1. 

“Over three miles I’m not saying we can overturn the form but I think we can give him more of a fight.

“He only beat us just over a length at Naas and three miles on a tougher track in the Albert Bartlett is the perfect race for him.

“He was doing his best work at the finish last time and the length and a bit he got beat by is the difference between a good and a bad jump at Cheltenham and it’s all about getting the luck in running.”

MENGLI KHAN - SUPREME NOVICES’ HURDLE

“His couple of early runs were very, very good. On form I can’t see how he can beat Getabird. 

“I was very impressed with him at Punchestown that day. There’s a rumour that he’s better going right-handed than left-handed so that’s the only thing I’m hoping for which might help me. 

“Better ground will suit Mengli Khan. There’ll be three or four of us swinging off the home turn going to the last and if my lad gets a clear round he’ll be one of them. If he’s good enough then the rest is history.

“I still think it will be a difficult job to beat Getabird but he’ll be a different horse at Cheltenham as he wasn’t quite on his top form the last time.”

FLAWLESS ESCAPE - MARTIN PIPE

“He’ll be heading for the Martin Pipe hopefully but we’ll wait and see for the weights.”

DOUNIKOS, JURY DUTY AND FAGAN - NATIONAL HUNT CHASE

“Dounikos is in the RSA and the four-miler but I think the four-miler is made for him.

“Fagan will go for it as well. He’s in good form at home and we’re going their fresh. He was second in the Albert Bartlett but he’s had a few problems. Hopefully those are behind him and he’s another who loves the sun on his back and the better ground. 

“Dounikos and Jury Duty both have Grade 1 form.”

FARCLAS - JCB TRIUMPH HURDLE

“He’ll definitely go for the Triumph, I think he’ll improve from Leopardstown and the better ground will suit him.”
