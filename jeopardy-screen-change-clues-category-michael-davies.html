Jeopardy! fans divided after bosses consider making major change to how clues are displayed

March 07, 2024
blog

JEOPARDY!'s top bosses have floated the idea of drastically changing how viewers see clues at home.

They've pitched keeping the category title above every clue that fills the screen once selected by a contestant.

Jeopardy!'s executive producer Michael Davies tossed around the dramatic change on his podcast Inside Jeopardy!.

It boiled down to: “In the era of HD television, whether or not we should be putting the category on the screen when the clues appear.”

“Because very often we don't cut back to the wide shot of the board when players select and clues pop out.

"People select so fast and they often don't say the name of the category.

"Even I get lost in terms of what category we're in sometimes," the head honcho said.

"And I know that's part of it for the advanced Jeopardy! viewer - keeping up."

But, he wondered: "Should we find screen real estate to put the category up every time - what do you all think about that?"

The addition would remind fans of the topic, but the clues would still look the same for contestants on stage.

When a clue is picked, contestants see the entire game board on stage - the clues only take up the entire screen for viewers.

Longtime Producer Sarah Foss agreed this idea could work for "text clues" but would “take away from the experience” for video-based clues, she said.

They agreed they would try it with a tournament first and are having their team work up a "mock version."

FANS ARE SPLIT

On Reddit, fans wasted no time and mocked up a version of their own (seen below).

Many fans in the thread felt this could be an improvement for keeping up with the lightning-fast game, but others weren't so sure.

As Davies admitted, hardcore fans like the challenge of not being hit over the head with a category name clue-by-clue.

And the extra text would clutter the screen and could arguably make things visually worse, not better.

Long category names - like "Where Did I Leave My Keys?" from a recent episode - would see the screen cramped with text when each of its five clues are picked.

One user wrote: "This would definitely help at home. Categories like 'Countries that start with N' and my dumb a** answers Bolivia. Then I'm like, Duh!'"

Another agreed: "They fit the entire Final Jeopardy clue AND category in a small fraction of the space. It really shouldn't be a problem."

"But people have 30 seconds to read that," replied a third, seeming to think the screen may be too crowded if so.

Another similarly feared: "Sometimes the categories are long though."

And a fourth also worried: "You have to be paying attention in order to answer correctly [at home]," - and that's undoubtedly part of the charm.

One commenter defended: "Does that matter? I have horrible eyesight and I can still read it in about a second."

And another: "Bear in mind that the contestants don't see the clue as we do at home.

"I don't think anyone is talking about changing it on stage. Just the broadcast.

"For contestants on stage, the categories are always visible to them.

"I think having the category the same font and size as the clue while underlined would be perfect," that user concluded.

'NOBODY GET UPSET!'

Over the summer, Ken Jennings, 48, and actress Mayim Bialik were officially announced as the permanent replacements for the late, great Alex Trebek.

Mayim is about to replace Ken in just days for a new Teen Reunion Tournament which will then see her host more regular episodes afterward.

Davies has also launched the new Second Chance competition, ramped up the show's social media presence, and debuted the hour-long Celebrity Jeopardy! tournament with a "Triple Jeopardy" round in prime time - which had a mixed reception.

Davies has also launched Jeopardy! Masters in primetime, which Ken will be hosting this spring.

But he has also floated ideas that have crashed and burned gameplay-wise, like adding a cash bonus to regular games, which was so divisive the show decided against it.
