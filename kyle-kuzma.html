Kyle Kuzma: Early Life

Kyle Alexander Kuzma, famously known as Kyle Kuzma, is an American basketball professional who dons the Washington Wizards’ jersey at the NBA.

Born and raised in Michigan, he is the son of Karri Kuzma, his mother, a high-school shotput champion.

Along with his mother and stepfather, Kuzma spent his childhood with two step-siblings. 

Kuzma’s mother and stepfather had put a toy basketball rim in the living room for Kuzma. And this ignited Kuzma’s passion and interest in the game when he was just two years old. 

Likewise, young Kuzma attended Bentley High School in Michigan. As a junior in high school, Kuzma averaged 17.9 points, 14.4 rebounds, and 3.8 assists per game.

Rise Academy in Philadelphia brought Kuzma after looking at his shooting tapes. 

After high school, Kuzma was offered to play with Division I schools like Iowa State and Missouri. However, he decided to be a part of the University of Utah. 

College Life

Kuzma played basketball for Utah Utes during his years at college. However, he started his basketball career at Utah only in his sophomore year. He signed late for college and was, thus, redshirted for his freshman year. 

During his sophomore year, Kuzma was a starter in the playing lineup for Utah. The player averaged 10.8 points per game this season. 

In the 2016-2017 season, he was named the first-team all-conference in the Pac-12 in his junior year at college. He averaged 16.4 points, 2.4 assists, and 9.3 rebounds per game in his junior year. 

After giving consistent performances for Utah, he got selected in the first round of the 2017 NBA draft. He became the 27th overall pick in the draft. 

Kyle Kuzma graduated with a degree in sociology from the University of Utah.

Kyle Kuzma: Career

The Brooklyn Nets selected Kuzma in the first round of the NBA draft in 2017. The Los Angeles Lakers conducted a draft-day trade with the Nets to get Kuzma to the Lakers. 

Likewise, the 2017-2018 NBA season was a rookie season for Kuzma as a part of the Los Angeles Lakers. 

Kuzma started in six of the seven games he played for the Lakers in the NBA Summer League of 2017. During all seven games, he became a consistent performer for the team. 

There Kyle averaged 21.9 points, 6.4 rebounds, and 2.7 assists per game. He was consequently named to the All-Summer League Second Team.

And was also named the Summer League Championship Game MVP after the championship match against the Portland Trail Blazers.

In October and November 2017, Kuzma was named the Western Conference Rookie of the Month. 

Kuzma became the first-ever rookie in the NBA to compile over 330 points, 120 rebounds, and 30 three-pointers in just his first 20 games.

In the game against Houston on December 20, Kuzma scored a career-high of 38 points. His performance also helped to end the Rockets’ 14-game winning streak. 

He achieved a unique feat with the Lakers soon. He scored 25 or more points in three consecutive games, making him the first Lakers rookie since Jerry West in 1961.

Kuzma scored a new career-high against the Pistons in October 2018, scoring 41 points. 

The Rise of Kyle Kuzma

Kuzma’s rise began in the NBA when his performances named him to the NBA All-Rookie First Team with the Lakers in 2018. 

The Michigan-born scored a match-winning three-pointer in August 2020 to get the Lakers over the line against the Nuggets. 

That same year, he won an NBA Championship with the Lakers. Soon after, he was traded to the Wizards.

Furthermore, in November 2021, Kuzma scored four 3-pointers in just the fourth quarter against the Cavaliers. Two of them were scored in the final 30 seconds to get the Wizards over the line.
