Madeleine Astrid Gurdon, Baroness Lloyd-Webber, is a prominent English equestrian and the wife of the renowned theatrical impresario, Andrew Lloyd Webber. Born on November 30, 1962, into a military family, she grew up with three sisters, and her father, Brigadier Adam Gurdon, had a distinguished military career.

Madeleine's life story is one of remarkable achievements and diverse experiences, encompassing her upbringing, and equestrian career. She is not only known for her equestrian accomplishments but also for her philanthropic work, including her role as the president of the Pony Club. Her personal life includes a successful marriage to Andrew Lloyd Webber, with whom she has three children.

Early Life and Family Background

Madeleine Gurdon comes from a military family and has three sisters. Her father, Brigadier Adam Gurdon, served in Korea and later in Kenya during the Mau Mau Uprising.

The Gurdon family's life was characterized by extensive travel, including stints in Cyprus, Tanganyika, and Hong Kong. Madeleine received her education at a convent school.

Who Is Madeleine Gurdon's Husband?

Madeleine Gurdon's personal life is intertwined with her marriage to the famous theatrical impresario, Andrew Lloyd Webber. The married couple exchanged vows on February 9, 1991, and their enduring marriage has lasted for over three decades.
