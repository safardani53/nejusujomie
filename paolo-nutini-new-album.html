Paolo Nutini announces his first album in eight years as he returns with two new songs

June 26, 2024
blog

YOU wait eight years for a new song from Paolo Nutini, then two come along at once.

The Scottish star made his grand return to music last night when he dropped a pair of singles and announced a brand new album — his first since 2014’s Caustic Love.

But he made sure to keep fans waiting, originally saying they would be out at 7.30pm but then not releasing them until much later.

The record, his fourth studio album, will be called Last Night In The Bittersweet.

It has been described as a 70-minute epic spanning “from classic rock to post-punk”.

His first two singles, called Lose It and Through The Echoes, give fans an ample taste of what to expect from the record.

But they could barely be more different from each other, suggesting it will be his most eclectic album to date — and quite the departure from the sound he became known for on his first three albums.

The delay to the releases was believed to be due to Paolo making sure he was 100 per cent happy with the tracks, which have been years in the making.

A source said: “Paolo has spent so long working on this new music that he has been extra hands-on in making sure everything is completely perfect.

“He cares so much about the songs and wants to give his fans, who have waited years for new music, the best experience possible.

“Paolo was insistent that only a handful of people in his inner circle listened to the songs before they were released.

“And hardly anyone has heard the album yet.

“He is a very private person and wasn’t going to risk anything leaking at the last moment.

“Things have been kept under lock and key for months but now he is finally ready to release it.”

He first shot to fame in 2006 with his love song Last Request and went on to have hits including Jenny Don’t Be Hasty, Pencil Full Of Lead and Scream (Funk My Life Up).

But his gravelly voice hasn’t been heard for years, as he hasn’t performed — and has barely been seen — since going on hiatus in 2017.

He will return to the stage this Saturday with a performance at the 900-capacity Leadmill in Sheffield, followed by further intimate shows at central London’s 100 Club and Corran Halls in Oban in his native Scotland.

Tickets for those shows sold out in seconds earlier this week but he will also play a string of festivals this summer.

As one of the nation’s best singer-songwriters, it’s good to have him back.

CHARLI’S TO-ITALY IN TO GEORGE

CHARLI XCX has confirmed her romance with The 1975’s George Daniel as they jetted off on a romantic holiday together.

The Used To Know Me singer shared photos on Instagram of them on the trip, which she said was “delicious”.

They are believed to have flown to Italy for the getaway ahead of the European leg of her Crash world tour, which starts in Dublin tomorrow.

The couple first met when they worked on a song called Spinning, which was released last spring. Then they teamed up to make her album Crash, which was released in March.

George co-wrote and produced the title track and persuaded her to end the record with her song Twice. She explained: “George Daniel, who is very good with tracklisting, simply said, ‘You’re an idiot if you don’t put this song last’.”

Despite his less-than-charming language, they later grew close following the breakdown of her long- term relationship with video game producer Huck Kwong last year.

Now they are quite the power couple, with 22 Top 40 hits and five No1 albums between them.

But with The 1975 expected to return with even more new music this year, organising their diaries so they actually have time to see each other will be quite the mission.

LIAM BLASTS WOKE BANDS

WE’VE had our differences over the years but I can totally get behind Liam Gallagher on this one.

The former Oasis legend has blasted the idiotic woke brigade and says new musicians are too afraid to be hell-raisers for fear of being cancelled.

When asked about his thoughts on cancel culture, Liam said: “Who the f*** are these people cancelling you, anyway?

“Like, f*** off. Unless they come to your house and say you’re cancelled, you’re still going to do your thing.

“They don’t speak for everyone, do they?

“The cancelling people just speak for the cancelled world.

“You can still do your gig — there are people out there who are going to like what you say. Bring it f***ing on, you f***ing squares!” Speaking on The 2 Johnnies Podcast, Liam also admitted he believed the shift in culture had left young rock stars too afraid to be controversial, as he was during his heyday.

He added: “No one is really blowing my mind, if I’m honest.

“I just think this world now — there’s a lot of people who are scared to say what they want to.  They’d get cancelled.

“I’d hate to think I was the last rock star, because that’s just f***ing sad.

“I’ve got a problem with a lot of these bands today who go, ‘I’m an artist’.

“No you’re f***ing not. You’re a geezer in a band, so shut the f*** up.

“They’re pretentious, talking about the artistry and all that.

“Behave, man.”

REBEL’S BACK IN SCHOOL

REBEL WILSON is looking good after shedding 5st — and now she is going back to school for her latest role.

The Aussie actress plays cheerleader Stephanie Conway, who wakes up after a 20-year coma, in Netflix film Senior Year.

Rebel posed on the red carpet at the movie’s premiere in Los Angeles on Tuesday, wearing this emerald dress.

I’D GO ZAC TO MUSICAL ROLE

ZAC EFRON has revealed he’d love a High School Musical reboot.

The actor starred in the original 2006 Disney Channel film opposite Vanessa Hudgens — as well as the sequels in 2007 and 2008 — and wants to bring back his character Troy Bolton.

He told E News: “To have an opportunity in any form to go back and work with that team would be so amazing. My heart is still there. That would be incredible. I hope it happens.”

Since then he has starred in big-budget movies including The Greatest Showman, Baywatch and Hairspray.

But with High School Musical being one Disney Channel’s most successful franchises, I’m sure moves for a reboot will be made in no time now Zac is keen — whatever the cost.
