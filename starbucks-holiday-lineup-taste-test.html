I tried Starbucks' new Holiday menu - and it's bad news for the calorie counters, but it's worth it

July 10, 2024
blog

IT'S red cup season at Starbucks, and that means the holiday season has officially commenced.

Alongside the holiday cups, Starbucks launched its holiday lineup today, featuring favorites like the Peppermint Mocha, Caramel Brulee Latte and Chestnut Praline Latte.

Other holiday treats on the menu this year include: 

  • Toasted White Chocolate Mocha
  • Irish Cream Cold Brew
  • Iced Sugar Cookie Almondmilk Latte
  • Chocolate Pistachio Swirl (a new bakery item this year)
  • Reindeer Cake Pop
  • Sugar Plum Cheese Danish
  • Cranberry Bliss Bar
  • Snowman Cookie

Here at The U.S. Sun, we decided to taste some of the top Starbucks holiday drinks. 

We set out to determine which beverages were truly worth the cost and the calories.

Starbucks offers its hot drinks in short (eight fl oz), tall (12 fl oz), grande (16 fl oz), venti (20 fl oz).

Read more about Starbucks

With the average Pumpkin Spice Latte coming in at 390 calories, it’s safe to say that Starbucks hasn’t always been known to create beverages with your health and wellness in mind.

The chain's holiday beverages are no different. But there are specific strategies you can use to get a delicious drink without the nutritional dilemma.

Taste is also vital in your everyday coffee drink, so I went ahead and reviewed a few of the holiday items for their overall quality as well.

For this test, I ordered the grande.

Peppermint Mocha (4 out of 5)

  • Calories: 440
  • Price: $6.65

The Peppermint Mocha first debuted in 2002 and quickly became one of the most beloved beverages on Starbucks’ menu. 

It’s espresso combined with steamed milk, mocha sauce and peppermint-flavored syrup, topped with whipped cream and dark chocolate.

When looking at the macronutrients, it’s a whopping 440 calories with four pumps of peppermint syrup and four pumps of mocha.

However, I found this to be the perfect traditional holiday drink.

I would not order it every day, but if you’re shopping for Christmas presents or spending time with loved ones this season, it’s a go-to drink.

The rich chocolate flavor pairs well with a slight peppermint edge, and it ushers in a specific kind of sweetness that reminds me of holidays past.

Overall, I give this beverage a four out of five.

It’s a quality beverage, but in total, it's a little unhealthy for everyday holiday season drinking.

Chestnut Praline Latte (4.5 out of 5)

  • Calories: 330
  • Price: $6.65

If you prefer a more nutty flavor, the Chestnut Praline Latte is the holiday flavor for you. 

It features espresso, steamed milk, and the flavors of caramelized chestnuts and spices topped with whipped cream and spiced praline crumbs.

Nutritionally, this coffee might be a bit better for you at just 330 calories.

That’s still a large amount given that so many people drink a latte every morning, and the average person is recommended to eat around 2,000 calories per day.

However, I have to say, this was my favorite drink of the bunch.

Its rich caramel flavor was something I can see myself craving on a regular basis, and it was a perfect mix of sweet but nutty.

As my favorite holiday drink I tried, I rate it a 4.5.

Iced Sugar Cookie Almond Milk Latte (3 out of 5)

  • Calories: 180
  • Price: $6.65

Customers were eager to embrace the Iced Sugar Cookie Almond Milk Latte last year. 

It’s a coffee concoction of sugar cookie syrup, Starbucks Blonde Espresso, ice, and almond milk. 

Naturally, the drink is topped with festive red and green sprinkles.

This is by far the healthiest coffee of the group at only 180 calories, which we can likely attribute partly to its almond milk over natural dairy.

However, because its sugar content is lower than the other drinks we taste tested, it definitely did not have as powerful of a flavor.

It felt a little watered down even though traditionally I prefer iced lattes over their warm versions.

This would be a good drink to get on a regular basis because it is a lot healthier than the others, but it didn’t live up to my expectations for a holiday drink.

Because of this, the beverage only earns a three out of five.

Chocolate Pistachio Swirl (2.5 out of 5)

  • Calories: 280
  • Price: $4.15

New this year, the Chocolate Pistachio Swirl is a pastry item filled with creamy chocolate, with notes of cinnamon and coffee to perfect the flavor. 

The entire pastry comes out at 280 calories, but I can give that a slide because the pastry was actually far larger than I was expecting.

However, I ultimately give the pastry a 2.5 because it lags behind on flavor.

I longed for more chocolate filling, and the pastry was more doughy bread than I preferred.

Counting calories

Calorie count vary depending on what you order.

The other Starbucks holiday drinks and bakery items and their calorie information is listed as follows:

  • Caramel Brulee Latte: 410 calories
  • Toasted White Chocolate Mocha: 420 calories
  • Irish Cream Cold Brew: 200 calories
  • Reindeer Cake Pop: 140 calories
  • Cranberry Bliss Bar: 240 calories
  • Sugar Plum Cheese Danish: 330 calories
  • Snowman Cookie: 390 calories

Tips for making your drink healthier at Starbucks

While the majority of Starbucks holiday drinks have a high calorie and sugar content, you can modify your order to best fit your own nutritional needs.

For instance, you can substitute skim or almond milk to lighten up the calories for the creamer in your latte.

Additionally, Starbucks always lets you customize the number of flavor pumps in your drink.

For the Peppermint Mocha and Chestnut Praline Latte, these tend to add the heaviest number of calories, so if you get two versus four in your Grande, you will be on a better pathway to health.

Just remember, it might lower the richness of the flavor.

Starbucks also offers low-calorie drinks like the Nitro Cold Brew and Vanilla Sweet Cream Cold Brew for under 100 calories.

The Vanilla Sweet Cream Cold Brew is a great option for those who want something sweet but don’t want to splurge on the sugar and extra calories that accompany a traditional Starbucks drink, since it’s only 70 calories for a Grande.

Lastly, remember Starbucks has sugar-free vanilla syrup as well as calorie-free sweeteners like Stevia or Splenda to make you feel like you’re drinking a sweet drink without the nutritional impact.
