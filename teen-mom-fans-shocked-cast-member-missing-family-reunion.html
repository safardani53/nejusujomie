Teen Mom fans shocked after key cast member is missing from new Family Reunion trailer

March 30, 2024
blog

TEEN Mom fans have expressed shock after a key cast member is missing from the new Family Reunion trailer.

MTV released the season two trailer for Family Reunion on the show's official Instagram as well as its other social media platforms.

The preview showcased scenes from the upcoming season of Teen Mom: Family Reunion and much of the expected drama that occurred during its filming.

MTV producers also added an interesting twist to the cast members' second outing, by inviting each of their mothers to join their daughters in attendance.

Many fan favorites appeared within the brief runtime of the video, including Maci Bookout, 31, Cheyenne Floyd, 30, and Catelynn Lowell, also 30.

One notable figure missing from the trailer was Teen Mom 2 standout, Leah Messer, 30.

Fans were quick to point out that the mother-of-three was absent from the preview, with one person commenting: "Where's Leah?"

Teen Mom fans on Reddit also questioned Leah's absence.

One Redditor asked: "So does this mean Leah is no longer a part of the [Teen Mom] franchise?

Another person wrote: "No Leah Messer?"

While a third fan theorized: "I’d be surprised if Mama Dawn does any more substantial work with TM since Leah’s book came out."

Back on Instagram, one in-the-know fan responded: "She didn't attend," which prompted another person to comment: "That's a bummer! She's one of my favorites."

'HOT MESS'

Another Teen Mom 2 alum, Briana DeJesus, 28, has said the upcoming season of the spinoff show Family Reunion is a "hot mess express."

Production was previously halted after she and her co-star Ashley Jones, 25. got into a nasty fight.

Posting to Twitter last week, the Teen Mom: The Next Chapter star wrote: “Season finale coming up only means Teen Mom: Family Reunion will start soon.”

She warned her fans: “And that s**t was a hot mess express,” adding two laughing emojis

Although the cast for the new season has yet to be announced, fans are already bracing themselves for drama.

The U.S. Sun previously reported how a fight broke out between Briana and Ashley and their mothers, Roxanne and Tea Chapple, during the filming of Family Reunion in August.

According to The Ashley’s Reality Roundup, the fight lasted several days and resulted in the production crew going on strike.

Per the outlet, Ashley spat on her cast member during a bitter disagreement while filming.

Members of the camera crew and sound technicians subsequently put down their equipment at the resort in Oregon where the spin-off was being filmed. 

The fight began during one of the first cast meals, a source told The Ashley’s Reality Roundup.

The cast was allegedly having a conversation about supporting one another on social media when things got ugly. 

‘IT GOT PHYSICAL’

"Roxanne made a comment about how the grandmas from the show need to be supportive of each other and [Tea] took it as a jab at her," an insider said. 

"She told Roxanne to say what she wanted to say, so Roxanne started talking about some of the stuff Tea has said about Briana on social media."

The source added: "They started screaming and cussing at each other and tried to get at each other but it was broken up by security."

However, that wasn't the end of the drama.

Several sources alleged that things got even more intense later that evening inside the resort.

"Ashley and Tea walked up on [Roxanne and Briana] in the kitchen and they all started screaming at each other," one source alleged.

"That's when it turned physical. Ashley spit on Briana, and then someone started throwing stuff. It got more physical before it was finally broken up. But all four of the women were fighting each other.

Briana has since addressed the fight on social media and warned Ashley she will be taking legal action.

LEAH'S REMARKS

While the TV personality didn't attend the filming of Family Reunion, Leah has been involved in some drama of her own since her split from ex-fiance Jaylan Mobley.

The MTV star took to her Instagram Stories last week to throw shade at her ex, by reposting a scathing tweet she'd previously shared.

The tweet read: "NO approval or validation is ever needed when it comes to YOUR life, your BOUNDARIES, and what your heart DESIRES. Let this be known and if it isn't respected, stand UP for yourself."

And in her Instagram post, she added: "I would like to add that in the dinner scene after the proposal it was known that no other blessings were needed or mattered especially if I was going to open myself to marriage after two failed marriages.

"I love my family BUT I have a very private relationship with them and preferred to keep it that way as we build better relationships with healthier boundaries."

Leah continued: "I call the scene with my stepdad a publicity stunt and disrespect at its finest when you asked me prior and proceeded to do so anyway… then tell me on camera after you propose. Smh.

"You deserve better, never settle," the mom-of-three added.

Leah and Jaylan got engaged in August when he popped the question with a 4.7-carat custom ring during their anniversary trip to Costa Rica.
