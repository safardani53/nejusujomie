UK earthquake of 4.4 magnitude with epicentre outside Swansea felt across Wales and western England

July 25, 2024
blog

BRITAIN has been hit by it's biggest earthquake in a decade as 'terrified' homeowners felt tremors across several hundred miles.

The British Geological Society confirmed a 4.4-magnitude earthquake struck near Swansea at around 2.30pm this afternoon.

A spokesman said tremors had been felt as far away as Birmingham and Devon.

Hundreds of people across south-west England and Wales say they felt their homes and workplaces shake. The most serious tremors were felt in South Wales area.

It is the biggest earthquake to hit the UK since the 5.2-magnitude quake in Market Rasen, Lincolnshire, in 2008.

There have been no reports of serious damage or injuries. One person was hurt by a toppled chimney in Barnsley, South Yorks.

Dyfed-Powys Police said it has received an "extremely high call volume" following the quake.

South Wales Police described it as a 'minor earthquake' and urged people not to contact emergency services if not reporting damage or injuries.

Port Talbot FC stopped their Welsh Football League match in the 43rd minute after feeling the tremors.

The club said on Twitter: "43' - temporary stop in play. A recorded earthquake in the area. Incredible."

Swansea University's Bay Campus was evacuated for a short time following the tremors

Michael Sheen’s dad Meyrick Sheen, who lives in Baglan, near Port Talbot, told Wales Online: "We have lived here for 40 years and we have never experienced anything like this before, it felt like a massive explosion under ground.

"The walls shook, the lights flashed and the pictures were shaking. It was frightening."

James Leonard wrote: "Wow was that an earthquake in Swansea my house just shook #earthquake earthquake."

Alex Morss added: "This sounds strange, but I think I just felt an #earthquake right under me here in Bristol, UK?!

"I was sat writing in the loft when the table, computer and whole floor shook for a couple of seconds. There was a big thud that felt like something had crashed."

The largest known British earthquake occurred in the North Sea near the Dogger Bank in 1931, with a magnitude of 6.1.

The most destructive UK earthquake hit the Colchester area in April 1884. At least two people were killed and 1,250 buildings were damaged.
