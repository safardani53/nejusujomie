What are the dangers of Xbox gift cards?

While it’s often said that giving gift cards is “impersonal,” it turns out most people are just fine with that, as they remain incredibly popular with customers.

People enjoy the flexibility and convenience of gift cards.

Unfortunately, gift cards are prime targets for scammers and fraudsters, and gift card fraud can leave merchants vulnerable to damaging chargebacks from the credit cards that were originally used to purchase them. 

With physical gift cards, which are still the more popular option, the person receiving one has to make sure to register it on time.

Otherwise, the card may easily be lost or stolen, and it can be very difficult to get a refund if it hadn't been registered.

While Microsoft assures users that gift cards redeemable in their store never expire, there is always a danger of losing the money if the company goes out of business, for example.

Another thing to be aware of is that while Xbox gift cards don't expire, promotional gift cards such as those from Microsoft Rewards or Xbox Live Rewards do.

Are there any Xbox gift card scams to watch out for?

In July 2021, Bloomberg reported about an elaborate Xbox gift card scam, where a Microsoft employee stole more than $10 million from the US giant.

Volodymyr Kvashuk found a glitch where he could generate gift card codes and trade them for Bitcoin, which he then turned to cash.

Scammers often send emails which appear to be from the official Microsoft account but are intended to steal personal data.

Although elaborate scams like that do exist, there are ways to remain smart about your gift card purchases online.

Hang Up on Fake Callers

No reputable company nor government agency (including the IRS) will ever demand payment with gift cards. If someone claims to be from the IRS, hang up. (Don’t believe emails either.)

Check the Email address

Look closely at the email address sending the gift card, and make sure it's coming from @microsoft.com address.

Balance Check in Private

If someone asks to listen as you call to confirm the balance of a gift card, it is likely a scam.

Inspect Gift Card Packaging

If the packaging looks tampered with or the PIN is revealed, turn the gift card into the cashier and pick a different card.

Check the Activation Receipt

Be sure the gift card number listed on the activation receipt matches the gift card you receive. Alert the manager if it’s not a match.
